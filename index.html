// About Me
A diligent scientist driven by
curiosity and real-world impact

I am a Kenyan-born climate scientist and geophysicist currently pursuing my PhD at AIMS Research and Innovation Center in collaboration with Imperial College London, where I develop multi-sensor deep learning frameworks for flood depth estimation in Rwanda's mountainous cities.

My academic journey spans four countries — Kenya, Senegal, Rwanda, and the UK — earning me a BSc with First Class Honours in Education Science (Physics/Mathematics), an MSc in Mathematical Sciences with Distinction from AIMS-Senegal, and an MSc in Geophysics from ICTP-EAIFR.

Beyond research, I bring hands-on professional experience in IT leadership, teaching, digital transformation, student governance, peer counselling, and community service — from training children in computer programming at Don Bosco Karen to chairing university electoral commissions.

// Research
Current & Past Work
Current · PhD Research

Multi-Sensor Deep Learning Framework for Flood Depth Estimation in Mountainous Cities

AIMS-RIC / Imperial College London · Oct 2025 – Present

Integrating SAR time-series, optical Earth observation, and terrain metrics to estimate flood depth in Musanze and Rubavu, Rwanda. The framework aims to improve early-warning systems and disaster resilience in vulnerable mountainous urban environments.

Completed · MSc Thesis (Geophysics)

Time Series InSAR Analysis over the Olkaria Geothermal Power Plant in Kenya: Insights From a 2020–2024 Sentinel-1 InSAR Survey

ICTP-EAIFR, University of Rwanda · 2023 – 2024

Conducted time-series Interferometric Synthetic Aperture Radar (InSAR) analysis using Sentinel-1 data to monitor ground deformation at Kenya's Olkaria geothermal field over a four-year period, providing insights into subsurface geothermal dynamics and surface displacement patterns.

Completed · MSc Dissertation

Atmospheric Rivers and Surface Melting over the Pine Island Glacier in 1983 and 1998

AIMS-Senegal · 2021 – 2022

Investigated the role of atmospheric rivers in driving surface melting events over the Pine Island Glacier in West Antarctica, analysing climate dynamics across two significant melt years. Completed as a Mastercard Foundation Scholar, graduated with Distinction.
